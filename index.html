The City Of Louisville

History

 Louisville was founded by George Rogers Clark in 1778. He was an American soldier, and a officer from Virginia who became the highest ranking American military officer on the northwestern frontier during the American Revolutionary War. He served as leader of the militia in Kentucky throughout much of the war. The city of Louisville was named after King Louis XVI of France, he was the last king of France before the monarchy in the French Revolutionary war. Louisville is the 29th largest city in the United States, We rank over some of the more popular cities such as Las Vegas, Portland, Atlanta, Miami. The population Of Louisville is 619,287. Louisville has several things that it is known for such as the Kentucky Derby, Thunder over Louisville, Ky State fair which is held in Louisville, and many more. Did you know that Thunder over Louisville is the biggest firework showing in North America? Thunder Over Louisville is an annual light show that is displayed every year in April kick start The Kentucky Derby. The show last about hour full of beautiful fireworks that are shown over the Ohio River. Downtown Louisville is filled with museums and different science centers, this area is known as "Museum Row". The "Museum Row" is located on Main St. and its filled with history on what Louisville is made of.

Sports

 Louisville is one of the greatest sports cities in the world, although we don't have a major sports team we do have an excellent university that we do root for night in night out. Louisville has some of the most loyal fans in the world. No matter the record Louisville fans cheer for their Cardinals. The University of Louisville has produced some of the all time great teams and players within the last few decades. In 2013 the Louisville Mens Basketball team won a NCAA National Championship. That was the first championship since the 1985-1986 season. The mens basketball team has a total of 3 NCAA championships, 10 Final Four appearances, and 19 conference tournament championships. So our mens program has been quiet successful. Our Women's basketball program has also had great success as well. They've been to several Final Fours and have made to the championship game but have fallen short each time. The football team has had its recent struggles in past seasons, but has been a power house over the past decade. The Cardinals produce one of the most electrifying players in the country. Lamar Jackson was one of the best players to ever play for the University of Louisville football program. He is the only Heisman trophy winner for the cardinals. In his Heisman season he had a total of 50 plus touchdowns and over 4000 total yards in offense. We also have a great baseball team and soccer team that our fans support as well. So our University is well rounded in the athletics and in education. Louisville is also known for the most exciting two minutes in sports also known as the Kentucky Derby. The race began in 1875, the distance of the race is one and one-fourth of a mile long. The race is taken place at Church Hill Downs, which has been around since 1901.

Marquee People

 Louisville is the home of the late great Muhammed Ali formally known as Cassius Clay. Muhammed Ali was known for is great boxing skills and flamboyant attitude surrounding his boxing skills. He was also known for his activism for African Americans in the early 1970's. Muhammed Ali attended Central High School and won the olympics for his boxing skills in 1960. At 18 years old he won all four of his fights and brought home a gold medal in the light heavyweight division. Ali was also known for refusing to fight Vietnam War. He felt as if the people of Vietnam cause him no harm so why must he fight them, he practice Islam and felt that world should lead with love and peace and not war. Muhammed Ali has a museum in his honor called The Muhammed Ali Center. This museum has all of his artifacts and accomplishments throughout his life. On June 3, 2016 Muhammed Ali passed away in Scottsdale Arizona. Louisville has produced several stars in their own such as actress Jennifer Lawrence who has won several awards for her acting and has stared in many of movie. Louisville has also produced plenty of talent such as Rajon Rondo who won an NBA title, D'angelo Russell a former number two NBA draft pick, Devontae Parker a former University Of Louisville wide receiver who is currently playing for the Miami Dolphins. Louisville has a lot of great history and a lot of great people that makes this wonderful city to be apart of.
